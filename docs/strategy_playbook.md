## 1. Executive summary — read this even if you read nothing else

You asked for strategies that "make 30–40% return." Here is the uncomfortable, load-bearing finding:

> **Every one of the 14 verified strategy families came back `plausible_30_40pct_in_7day = FALSE`.**

That is not pessimism — it is the arithmetic of a **7-day, long-only, spot, drawdown-capped** window. A +35% week on long-only spot is only reachable through:
1. **Leverage** — *unavailable on spot*;
2. **All-in concentration** on one token catching a parabola — a coin-flip on regime luck, not edge. The same concentration that prints +35% in a good week prints a **>30% disqualifying drawdown** in a reversal week; or
3. **Survivorship / zero-cost backtest fantasy** — the famous "180% APR grid bot" ran 10× leverage + zero fees; the "400% opening-range-breakout" and "228% Keltner" numbers exclude costs entirely; any "30–40%/month rotation" claim is survivorship-inflated (dead coins silently dropped, +50–200% inflation).

**The binding constraint in this contest is the drawdown gate, not the return target.** In a field where most entrants blow the 30% gate chasing a hero number, a **flat-to-+10% week with DD <15% places well**. So the winning objective is the *opposite* of swinging for 30–40%:

- **(a)** clear the **≥7-trade minimum**,
- **(b)** keep **max DD ≤15%** so you survive to be ranked at all,
- **(c)** post a **modest positive number**.

This is also precisely why the recommendation does **not** drag you back to the no-edge scalp. The deployable pick (§6) is a fundamentally different animal — **1h/4h trend + pullback, long-only spot, RR≥2, vol-targeted** — versus the **1m fade/follow ICT scalp** that [findings.md §15](findings.md) proved has negative out-of-sample expectancy after friction. Same *primitives*, completely different *setup*.

**Honest expected outcome for the recommended strategy: a 7-day distribution of roughly −10% to +15%, centered near zero, dominated by whether the contest week trends up.** A clean risk-on week could plausibly print +10–15%. Plan for that, not for a moonshot.

## 2. Methodology & ranking criteria

Each family was scored for **this format's** binding constraints, in priority order:

1. **Long-only-spot executable** — no shorting / perps / funding leg (the BSC venue cannot short).
2. **Friction-robust** — survives a **~0.5–4% PancakeSwap round-trip** with **RR ≥ 2**. (Per [findings.md](findings.md), only RR≥2 configs survived even *perp* friction of ~0.14% round-trip; AMM friction is **3–7× worse**.)
3. **Stays inside the ≤15% / 30% DD gate.**
4. **Fits 1h/4h bars** and can produce **≥7 trades** in 7 days.
5. **Maximal reuse** of existing, tested `ictbot` primitives.

Evidence was credibility-graded: **academic > reputable practitioner > exchange/marketing**. Hype was actively distrusted — survivorship bias inflates crypto backtests **50–200%** (>58% of all listed tokens are "dead"; only 9 of the Dec-2016 top-100 remain in the top-100 in 2024), and nearly every "X%/month bot" claim assumes zero cost and/or leverage.

**The `ictbot` primitives referenced throughout** (all real, tested):
- **Indicators** [`src/ictbot/indicators/`](../src/ictbot/indicators/): `atr`, `delta`/CVD, `fvg`, `liquidity` (swing highs/lows + `get_next_liquidity_level`), `mss` (market-structure shift), `poi_order_block` / `poi_min_max`, `structure` (`find_swings`, `get_swing_bias`), `bias_sma` / `bias_slope`, `regime` (`atr_percentile_regime` → HIGH/LOW/NORMAL vol), `mitigation`, `risk` (`calculate_rr`), `tick`.
- **Strategy** [`strategy/ict_pro_max.py`](../src/ictbot/strategy/ict_pro_max.py): `ICTProMaxStrategy(bias_engine, poi_engine, strategy_mode, sl_anchor, structural_tp1_rr, sl_atr_mult, tp_atr_mult, skip_in_low_vol, fib_filter, …)`; `evaluate()` → 25-key dict with `entry`/`sl`/`tp`/`tp2`/`rr`/`confidence`.
- **Backtester** [`engine/backtest.py`](../src/ictbot/engine/backtest.py): `run_backtest(pair, bars, …, fee_per_side, slippage_per_side, start_idx, end_idx)` — walk-forward capable, with a friction model.
- **Risk caps** [`portfolio/caps.py`](../src/ictbot/portfolio/caps.py): `MaxOpenPositions`, `MaxConcurrentSameDirection`, `DailyLossLimit`, `MaxDrawdown`, `MaxLiveTradesPerDay`, `NearPriceDedup`, `NewsBlackoutCap`; `Account` tracks equity + drawdown.
- **Execution** [`exec/`](../src/ictbot/exec/): `broker.py` protocol, `Order` struct, `PaperBroker`, `binance_live`, `delta_live`. **No CMC adapter and no BSC/PancakeSwap broker exist yet** — these are the two build gaps (§8).

### #1 — Trend-Following Pullback (buy-the-dip in a confirmed uptrend) · Fit 7/10 · **SUPERSEDED**

> **Superseded by the Decision Update (top of doc).** The pullback *concept* is kept, but its **ICT POI/MSS/FVG entry implementation was dropped** — that machinery has negative OOS expectancy ([findings.md](findings.md) §13/§15). The deployable pick is now the pure trend signal (`bias_sma`+`bias_slope`) with a simple pullback-to-MA entry (§6 / B4). Read the rest of this entry for the trend-following rationale, not the ICT entry mechanics.

**Mechanism.** Two-stage long-only gate on liquid majors. *Stage 1 — trend filter:* only act when the asset is in a confirmed uptrend (higher-highs/higher-lows + price above a rising slow MA) and not in dead-low volatility. *Stage 2 — pullback trigger:* wait for a retrace into a demand zone / OTE discount, then buy the bullish reclaim. Structural stop just below the zone; take-profit at RR≥2; trail to break-even after +1R. Timeframe 1h entry with 4h/1d trend context.

**Evidence.**
| Source | Type | Cred | Claim |
|---|---|---|---|
| Liu & Tsyvinski, *Risks and Returns of Cryptocurrency*, RFS 34(6) 2021 | academic | high | Crypto momentum/trend is a robust, priced return predictor across horizons. |
| Liu, Tsyvinski & Wu, *Common Risk Factors in Cryptocurrency*, JoF 2022 | academic | high | A 3-factor (market/size/**momentum**) model prices the cross-section — momentum is a genuine factor, not noise. |
| Rohrbach, Suremann & Osterrieder, *Momentum and Trend Following for Currencies and Bitcoin*, SSRN 2949379 | academic | high | TSMOM/EMA trend systems are profitable on BTC; Sharpe ~1.2–1.7 pre-cost. |

**Realistic performance (after AMM costs).** ~**10–30%/yr in a favourable trending regime**, near-0 or negative in chop. Sharpe ~1.2–1.7 pre-cost, materially lower after. **Max DD held to ~5–15%** by the trend filter + structural stops + 0.5–1% risk/trade — which is exactly the property the contest rewards. Win-rate moderate; positive expectancy comes from RR≥2, not hit-rate.

**Crypto fit.** Best in a **broad risk-on uptrend with clean pullbacks**; the trend filter self-disables in downtrends/chop (sits in stables), which is the built-in drawdown defence.

**Codebase mapping.** *Reuse (~80%):* `bias_engine='swing'` (`structure.get_swing_bias`), `poi_engine='order_block'` (`poi_order_block.get_ob_poi`, `fib_filter=0.5`), `mss.get_ltf_mss`, `sl_anchor='structural'` + `structural_tp1_rr=2.0`, `regime.atr_percentile_regime` (`skip_in_low_vol=True`), `get_next_liquidity_level` for TP2, the full caps stack, `Account` DD tracking, the WFO backtester. *Build-new:* the CMC adapter + BSC broker (§8), a long-only switch, and a 100–200h MA confirmation.

**Verdict.** **The deployable choice.** Long-only-spot native, friction-robust at RR≥2, self-DD-controlling, and reuses the most tested code. Full spec in §6.

### #2 — Time-Series Momentum / MA Trend-Following (4h, long/flat) · Fit 6/10 · **RUNNER-UP**

**Mechanism.** Per coin: go long when trailing return is positive / price is above a moving average; otherwise sit flat (in stables). No counter-trend, no shorting. Diversify the long/flat signal across the whole ~8–10-coin basket — **the diversification is the edge**, not any single coin. Run on **4h** bars.

**Evidence.**
| Source | Type | Cred | Claim |
|---|---|---|---|
| Moskowitz, Ooi & Pedersen, *Time Series Momentum*, JFE 2012 | academic | high | TSMOM is a pervasive, significant effect across asset classes; diversified Sharpe ~1.3. |
| Man AHL, *In Crypto We Trend* (2024) | practitioner | high | 50/200-day trend on crypto: **single-coin Sharpe is small; a diversified multi-coin sleeve is where the Sharpe comes from.** |
| arXiv 2009.12155 (intraday BTC trend) | academic | high | **No profitable *intraday* BTC trend after costs → run the signal on 4h/daily, not 1h.** |

**Realistic performance.** Single-name ~0–30%/yr net; a diversified ~10-coin sleeve is steadier with a better Sharpe (CTA-industry-like **0.4–1.0 net** on daily signals). **Strategy-level DDs of 20–40% are normal** for unmanaged crypto trend (it gives back open profit on reversals) — so vol-targeting + `MaxDrawdown(0.15)` are mandatory.

**Crypto fit.** Sustained directional trends; weak in choppy ranges (whipsaw). The long/flat structure maps cleanly to long-only spot + the DD cap.

**Codebase mapping.** *Reuse:* `bias_sma`/`bias_slope` (signal **already shipped**; `strategy_mode='follow'` is the in-repo default that beat fade), `regime`, `NearPriceDedup`, caps, account. *Build-new:* basket scheduler on 4h + the shared CMC adapter / BSC broker. **Near-zero net-new strategy code.**

**Verdict.** **Lowest-build, highest-evidence backbone.** Pair it with #1's pullback entry, or fall back to it wholesale. Run on **4h, not 1h.**

### #3 — Dual Momentum (absolute + relative, with cash-out) · Fit 6.5/10 · **CO-PRIMARY FALLBACK**

**Mechanism.** Combine *absolute* momentum (only be in-market when the asset's own trailing return is positive — else **rotate to stablecoin**) with *relative* momentum (own the strongest 1–3 names). The cash-out leg is a native long-only risk-off switch — no shorting required.

**Evidence.**
| Source | Type | Cred | Claim |
|---|---|---|---|
| AUT/ACFR crypto TSMOM working paper | academic | high | Crypto time-series momentum with a risk-off filter improves risk-adjusted return materially. |
| Dobrynskaya, *Cryptocurrency Momentum and Reversal* (HSE/SSRN 3913263) | academic | high | Momentum is significant short-horizon; **reverses beyond ~1 month** — keep the holding window short. |
| Antonacci, *Dual Momentum Investing* (framework) | practitioner | medium | Absolute+relative momentum historically cuts drawdown vs. relative-only. |

**Realistic performance.** ~**0–30%/yr** for a well-run absolute+relative overlay on majors after costs; Sharpe 0.5–1.5. Classic monthly dual-momentum ran ~11% DD, but **concentrated crypto single-name with no intra-period stop can blow far past that** — keep it diversified + stopped.

**Crypto fit.** Trending markets with leadership rotation; the cash-out leg shines in downturns. Compressed to a 7-day window it loses some edge (it's a daily/weekly idea) but the risk-off behaviour is still valuable.

**Codebase mapping.** *Reuse:* `regime`, `atr`, caps, account. *Build-new:* a small cross-sectional ranker (rank trailing return across the basket) + the cash-out rule. **The non-trending-week insurance policy** for the #1 pick.

**Verdict.** Arm it as the **fallback for a chop week**: when #1's trend filter sits flat, rotate to the single strongest positive-momentum name or fully to stables.

**Mechanism.** Enter long when price expands beyond an ATR/volatility band or a prior range high (regime-shift capture). Wide ATR stops; trail with the band.

**Evidence.**
| Source | Type | Cred | Claim |
|---|---|---|---|
| Zarattini, Pagani & Barbon, *Catching Crypto Trends*, SFI RP25-80 (2025) | academic | high | Tactical breakout/trend on BTC + alts delivers strong risk-adjusted returns; diversified Donchian Sharpe ~1.5. |

### #5 — Grid / Range Trading (laddered mean-reversion) · Fit 6/10

**Mechanism.** Place a ladder of buy/sell levels across a defined range; profit from oscillation as price bounces between rungs. Naturally generates many fills.

**Evidence.**
| Source | Type | Cred | Claim |
|---|---|---|---|
| Stevens Institute FE800, *Improving Grid Trading Strategies in Bitcoin* | academic | medium | Grid market-making is profitable **in genuinely range-bound regimes**. |
| Bitsgap / Paybis backtesting writeups | practitioner | medium | Realistic-fee grid returns are modest; performance collapses out-of-range. |
| Bitsgap / Phemex on the breakout failure mode | exchange | high | **A trend breakout routinely produces 30–60%+ unrealized DD** without a hard range stop. |

**Realistic performance.** ~**8–25%/yr unleveraged in a true range**, flat-to-negative otherwise; Sharpe 0.5–1.5 in-regime. **Negative skew is the danger:** without a hard stop, one breakout blows the 30% gate. With a stop, capped ~≤15%.

**Crypto fit.** Only in a sideways week — and you cannot know in advance the contest week will range. **Gate-fragile.**

**Codebase mapping.** *Reuse:* `regime.atr_percentile_regime` as the *pause* gate (stop gridding when vol regime breaks), caps. *Build-new:* the grid engine + a **non-negotiable poll-and-exit range stop**.

**Verdict.** **Ranging-week backup only**, and only with a hard stop. Auto-satisfies the trade minimum, but the negative skew is exactly the wrong risk profile for a DD-gated contest.

### #6 — Short-Horizon Mean Reversion (RSI-2 / Bollinger / z-score) · Fit 6/10

**Mechanism.** Buy oversold dips on majors (RSI-2 low / lower-Bollinger / negative z-score), exit on reversion to the mean.

**Evidence.**
| Source | Type | Cred | Claim |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fičura (2023), *Size and volume on crypto momentum and reversal*, VSE FFA WP | academic | high | Short-term reversal exists — but **inverts to momentum on large, liquid, high-volume names** (exactly this universe). |

**Verdict.** **Ranging-week hedge only.** The reversal-flips-to-momentum result on liquid majors plus AMM friction makes it unreliable as a primary.

### #8 — Cross-Sectional Momentum / Relative-Strength Rotation · Fit 4/10

**Mechanism.** Each rebalance, rank the universe by trailing return and hold the top-K strongest, rotating periodically.

**Evidence.**
| Source | Type | Cred | Claim |
|---|---|---|---|
| Han, Kang & Ryu (2023), *TS & CS Momentum in Crypto under Realistic Assumptions*, SSRN 4675565 | academic | high | **CS momentum is the *weak* form** under realistic costs/liquidation; many significant-looking CS portfolios earn insignificant profit after reality. |
| Dobrynskaya (2024), *Crypto Momentum and Reversal*, SSRN 3913263 | academic | high | CS momentum works only on **2–4 week** horizons, then **reverses** and inflicts large losses. |
| Starkiller Capital, *CS Momentum in Crypto* | practitioner | medium | In-sample +69%/yr → **OOS −2.35%/yr**; DD **>75%**; 125bps round-trip makes it underperform the benchmark. |

**Realistic performance.** Liquid-majors long-only after costs: ~**0–25%/yr with wide variance**, many windows flat-to-negative. **Structurally high DD (40–75%)** without an absolute-momentum overlay. Positively skewed (a few winners carry it) — wrong profile for a 7-day total-return contest.

**Crypto fit.** Needs a broad trending market with clear leader/laggard dispersion; fails in chop and crashes on loser-rebounds.

**Codebase mapping.** *Reuse:* backtester friction model, ATR sizing, regime, caps. *Build-new:* a **cross-sectional ranker (does not exist** — current strategy is strictly single-pair) + rotation scheduler. **Most net-new code of any candidate.**

**Verdict.** Real effect, **wrong instrument for this window**: the 7-day/7-trade rule forces it off its only profitable 5–7d cadence into high-friction rotations, it carries genuine 30%+ DD gate-breach risk, and it needs the most new code.

### #9 — Donchian Channel / Turtle Breakout · Fit 4/10

**Mechanism.** Enter on an N-day high breakout, exit on the opposite channel. The classic Turtle system — profit comes from **holding winners across weeks** for the fat right tail.

**Evidence.**
| Source | Type | Cred | Claim |
|---|---|---|---|
| Zarattini, Pagani & Barbon (2025), *Catching Crypto Trends*, SFI RP25-80 | academic | high | Diversified multi-lookback **daily** Donchian on crypto: ~25–35% CAGR, Sharpe ~1.3–1.6, DD ~19–25%. |
| de Iriarte (2023), *Turtle Trading in Crypto* | practitioner | medium | Turtle works on crypto **over long daily samples**. |
| QuantPedia, *Trend-following & Mean-reversion in Bitcoin* | academic | medium | N-day breakout edges on BTC are real but **daily/multi-week**. |

**Realistic performance.** Daily ensemble ~25–35% CAGR / Sharpe 1.3–1.6 / DD 19–25% — **every figure is daily/multi-week.** Over a 7-day window of intraday single-name breakout: a bad week of whipsaws can be a string of −1R losses with no time for the right tail to pay.

**Crypto fit.** Excellent on daily horizons; **structurally amputated by a 7-day window** — its profit mechanism (let winners run for weeks) cannot operate.

**Codebase mapping.** *Reuse:* `atr`/`rr2plus` grids, liquidity levels, caps. *Build-new:* Donchian channel entry.

**Verdict.** Most academically credible of the breakout family, but **horizon-mismatched** to this contest. [findings.md](findings.md) also shows thin breakout edges die to friction at 7bps — well before the 7–15× worse AMM cost.

### #10 — Volatility-Targeting / Risk-Parity Sizing Overlay · Fit 4/10 (overlay)

**Mechanism.** Not a signal — a **sizing overlay**. Scale position inverse to realized volatility so exposure (and drawdown) stays stable; de-lever into high-vol regimes.

**Evidence.**
| Source | Type | Cred | Claim |
|---|---|---|---|
| Harvey, Hoyle, Rattray et al. (2018), *The Impact of Volatility Targeting*, FAJ | academic | high | Vol targeting improves Sharpe and **reduces drawdown** across asset classes. |

**Crypto fit.** Universally helpful as a risk overlay; on long-only spot it can only *de-lever* (forfeiting some of its documented gain), and a 7-day window is short for its edge to show.

**Codebase mapping.** *Reuse:* `atr`, `regime`, the sizing path, caps. *Build-new:* an inverse-vol scalar clamped ≤1.0.

**Verdict.** **Wire it as an overlay on the #1 pick**, not as a strategy — it cannot meet the ≥7-trade minimum alone, but it is cheap, evidence-backed DD-gate insurance.

## B0. What perps actually change

Two unlocks, two consequences:

1. **Shorting** revives the 4 families Part A had to exclude — funding-carry, basis, pairs/stat-arb, market-neutral momentum — plus net-new short-side directional plays. **But** every *market-neutral* family needs infrastructure the codebase lacks (see [B5](#b5-neutral-safe-satellite-not-the-engine)).
2. **Leverage** makes 30–40% in 7 days *arithmetically* reachable — and welds that upside to a symmetric, gate-lethal downside.

### The leverage → DD-gate math (memorize this table)

At **N×** leverage on a directional book, a **P% adverse move = N·P% drawdown**. The 30% disqualifier is therefore tripped by:

| Leverage | Adverse move that hits the 30% DQ | Reality check |
|:---:|:---:|---|
| **1×** | 30% | Spot-equivalent; only a major crash. |
| **2×** | **15%** | The "place, don't DQ" config. |
| **3×** | 10% | BTC/ETH do this intraday in a bad week. |
| **5×** (broker default) | 6% | A single ordinary session. |
| **8×** | 3.75% | One candle. |
| **10×** | 3% | One candle. |

**Leverage is Sharpe-neutral** (Van Hemert et al.): it manufactures *zero* risk-adjusted return. So the leverage you'd need to manufacture a 35% week (≈5–10×) is the exact knob whose downside disqualifies you on the first reversal. Feb-2026 printed a **−52% BTC day**; Oct/Nov-2025 saw **~$19–20B liquidation cascades**. At 5–10× those are not "drawdowns," they are liquidations.

## B3. Per-family deep dives (key facts + cited evidence)

### #1 Pairs / Statistical Arbitrage (cointegration long/short) — Score 5, DD **safe**
- **Mechanism.** Long/short a cointegrated pair (e.g. ETH/BTC, or an L1 basket) on the spread z-score; market-neutral, profit on mean-reversion of the spread.
- **Evidence.** *Statistical Arbitrage Using Cointegration in Crypto* (medium cred) — BTC-ETH pairs Sharpe ~2.45, ~16.3% annualized; *Pairs Trading in the Cryptocurrency Market* (Erasmus) — cointegrated pairs generate risk-adjusted excess returns but edge compresses with crowding.
- **Realistic.** ~8–20% annualized net of fees+funding for a disciplined book; **7-day +1–4%**, with a single cointegration breakdown turning the week −3% to −8%.
- **Codebase.** Reuse the SELL-side brackets + caps. **Build:** a two-leg/net-delta coordinator — `orders.py` models one pair/one side/one bracket, so a partial/failed fill on ONE leg leaves a **naked** position that silently destroys the neutrality the whole safety case rests on. **Riskiest build item.**
- **Verdict.** Safest *return-bearing* perp play — but venue-unverified and the two-leg coordinator is net-new.

### #2 Levered Trend-Following hybrid (long+short) — Score 4, DD **moderate** — ⏸ FUTURE WORK (was "THE PICK"; demoted — TWAK can't sign perps)
- **Mechanism.** Part A's trend-following core (`bias_sma`∧`bias_slope` agree) + pullback-to-MA entry, run on perps with the **short side** enabled (`Order.side=SELL`) when the 4h trend is down. RR≥2 floor. **ICT POI/MSS/FVG dropped** (no OOS edge).
- **Evidence.** [findings.md §12–16](findings.md) — the base ICT setup has **NO OOS edge** (only RR≥5 survived); He & Manela, *Fundamentals of Perpetual Futures* (arXiv 2212.06888) — realistic BTC perp Sharpe ~1.8 under retail-grade assumptions *when an edge exists*.
- **Realistic.** Unlevered, ~0 to slightly positive after 0.14% round-trip; at **2× / 0.5–1% risk / RR≥2 → −2% to +12%/7d**, centered near break-even-minus-funding.
- **Codebase.** **Lowest build** — mostly config: reuse 3-leg brackets + ISOLATED + BUY/SELL (testnet-proven, +5.02R XRP live), `follow`/`fade`, `bias_*`, `regime`, caps. **Build:** set `max_drawdown_frac≈0.15`; **add funding-cost accounting** (held 7-day perp pays ~0.2–1.5% funding, currently unmodeled); leverage/notional cap. *The real gap is EDGE, not execution.*
- **Verdict.** The balanced-levered choice. Full spec in [B4](#b4-the-balanced-levered-deployable-pick).

### #3 Short-Side Momentum / Downtrend Capture — Score 4, DD **dangerous**
- **Mechanism.** Short weak/breaking-down coins in a confirmed downtrend; you also *earn* funding shorting positive-funding coins.
- **Evidence.** Han, Kang & Ryu — TS momentum premium is **concentrated in winners**; the short-loser leg is risky. Drogen, Hoffstein & Otte — a 30-day winner/loser anomaly persists ~7 days, but the short side squeezes.
- **Realistic.** −10% to +25% standalone after fees; at 2× → −8% to +12%/7d.
- **Risk.** Reduce-only stop-market fills at the **squeeze candle**, not the trigger — backtests assuming stop-price fills understate left-tail DD. The single best squeeze-avoidance signal (funding feed) is **unbuilt**.
- **Verdict.** Cheap to add as the bearish half of #2, but the short tail is genuinely dangerous — keep leverage ≤2× and stops hard.

### #4 Levered Time-Series Trend-Following — Score 4, DD **dangerous**
- **Evidence.** Grayscale, *The Trend is Your Friend* — MA trend captures BTC upside while cutting tail exposure; arXiv 2602.11708 — best-case adaptive crypto trend Sharpe 2.41 / Calmar 3.18 / maxDD −12% (full cycle, **not** 7 days).
- **Realistic.** 15–40%/yr in trending regimes; a 7-day window is trend's *weakest* expression horizon. Funding-cost-blind in the current code.
- **Verdict.** Lowest-build directional, but the horizon mismatch + dangerous DD at the leverage needed to matter keep it behind #2.

### #5 Liquidation-Cascade Mean-Reversion — Score 4, DD **dangerous**
- **Mechanism.** Fade violent liquidation-driven wicks (mean-revert the over-shoot) with a deliberately **wide** stop and a time-based exit.
- **Evidence.** Curupira.dev (1m dual-filter velocity-z + 3× volume fade) — survives with tight risk control; Tigro Blanc (50k+ hours of data) — concluded the edge is marginal after costs.
- **Risk.** Wide stop = a continuation move is the dominant P&L driver = an outsized single loss is the core risk. The leverage needed to hit the target (8–15×) makes a normal 2–4% wick-extension a 16–60% DD.
- **Verdict.** Genuine microstructure edge (findings.md flagged it as a "next idea"), but the falling-knife tail makes it a research project, not a 7-day deploy.

### #6–14 (condensed)
- **VRP / Short-Vol & Funding (3):** delta-neutral carry ~8–16%/yr net at Sharpe 4–6 *but tiny absolute return*; the only contest-relevant form is 6–10× directional funding — a short-vol bomb. (Skyler Chan, SSRN leveraged funding-carry.)

## B4. The balanced-levered deployable pick

**→ Diversified Trend-Following hybrid (long + short), 2× on the BSC perp DEX.**

> The §6 trend-following core run on the **BSC perp DEX** with the **short side enabled** and **2× leverage** — long+short across the basket = **12–16 independent legs**. Same pandas-pure trend signal, same DD machinery; the ICT POI/MSS/FVG entries stay **deleted**.

**Entry.** Per coin on **4h**: long when `bias_sma` ∧ `bias_slope` agree up, short when both agree down (skip on disagreement / `LOW_VOL`). Timing = pullback to the bias-MA, enter on the in-trend resumption bar. Long via `Order.side=BUY`, short via `SELL`. **No POI/MSS/FVG/delta.**

**Exit.** Reduce-only `STOP_MARKET` SL (ATR-anchored ≈1.5–2.0×) + reduce-only `LIMIT` TP at ≥2R + **trend-flip exit** + trail-to-BE after +1R. Poll-and-exit (a DEX gaps between polls); `STOP_MARKET` fills at *market* on a gap, so realized loss scales 1:N with leverage — keep N low.

**Sizing & leverage.** ATR vol-targeting (`sizing.position_size`), **0.5–1% risk per leg**, **half-Kelly as an upper clamp only**. **Hard 2× cap** (3× absolute ceiling, never the broker 5× default — leverage is Sharpe-neutral). DD math (from [B0](#b0-what-perps-actually-change)): 2×→15%, 3×→10%, 5×→6% adverse move = 30% DQ.

**Expected outcome.** At 2× / 0.5–1% risk / RR≥2: **−4% to +12% over 7 days**, centered **+2–5%** trending / ~flat chop; fat right tail with shorts enabled in a clean directional week. DD-gate **safe by design** (vol-target + 2× cap + ATR stops + `MaxDrawdown` 0.15 + trend-flip exit). Will not DQ; the live risk is a chop week printing flat, not a blow-up.

**Codebase changes.** Reuse: `bias_sma`/`bias_slope`/`structure`, `atr`, `regime`, `sizing`, all caps, `Account`, `Order`/`PaperBroker` (`Side` already `BUY`/`SELL`; `realised_pnl_R` handles both directions). **Build-new:** `trend_basket.py`; the **strategy-agnostic WFO replay loop** (the gating item — `wfo.py` is POI-bound); the BSC perp DEX broker (long+short, reduce-only) + PancakeSwap spot broker, both signing via **TWAK**; the **funding feed + funding-accrual P&L** (`grep funding src/` = 0 hits; held perps pay ~0.2–1.5%/7d) + a long-side funding-skip; set `MaxDrawdown` 0.20→0.15 + the 2× clamp. **Delete from the live path:** the POI/MSS/FVG/delta AND-gate. **The gate is EDGE — validate via Gate A before levering; if it fails, ship the §6 spot long-only 1× fallback, never the leveraged variant unproven.**

## B5. Neutral-safe satellite (NOT the engine)

If you want a tiny DD-stabilizing sleeve alongside the directional driver, the structurally correct choice is **funding-carry / pairs**: near-zero directional drawdown by construction, the safest way to clear ≥7 trades and post positive. **But deploy it only as a small satellite, never as the engine** — its 7-day ceiling at safe leverage is **~0.2–2%**, it's **regime-dead right now**, and its *true* home is your broader always-on Binance/Delta yield product.

**Honest build cost (high, despite the short broker existing):** the SELL-side bracket is free, but you must build (1) a **funding-rate feed + cache** (zero funding refs in `src/`); (2) a **funding-percentile signal** (`ICTProMaxStrategy.evaluate()` is structure-based, not funding-based); (3) a **two-leg net-delta position + hedge-ratio rebalancer** (single-leg `Order`/3-leg bracket is the wrong shape); (4) **funding-accrual P&L** (`Account.book_close` is strictly R-multiple off SL distance — it *cannot* represent a no-SL carry position); (5) a negative-funding kill-switch.

## B6. The honest 30–40%-with-leverage verdict

**Yes, 30–40% in 7 days is now *arithmetically* possible — but it's closer to a coin-flip than a strategy.** Every market-neutral family is out as a *return engine* (their edge is collected on net notional, so leverage scales both legs equally — you'd need ~30–200× to reach the target, which isn't delta-neutral anymore, it's a liquidation bomb). So the target **forces a directional perp at ~5–10×**. At that leverage, a routine 3–6% session disqualifies you. Two compounding problems make it worse than the bare arithmetic: (1) [findings.md](findings.md) proves the directional signal has **~0 OOS edge** — leverage multiplies a ~0 edge by N while multiplying tail risk by N; (2) leverage is **Sharpe-neutral** — zero risk-adjusted return for the gate risk it imports. **Do not chase 30–40%.** The defensible play under a 15% self-cap is the **2× disciplined directional book targeting single-digit-to-~12% with a hard stop** — "place mid-pack, don't DQ" — which is exactly what [bnb_hackathon_plan.md](bnb_hackathon_plan.md) already concludes. If a competitor hits 35%, they did it by accepting a high probability of disqualification instead.
